Rules & Format

You must play all six rounds to be eligible to win the King Classic and have your name on the trophy. The winner will have the lowest net score over six rounds.

The highest score you can card is +8 on a hole. Putt everything out and play everything down. All standard rules apply, including the local rule that allows you to drop and take a two-stroke penalty anywhere perpendicular to where a ball is lost or crossed the out of bounds line. Check out this video for a more thorough explanation of the rule.

Initial handicaps must be verified, but do not need to be official. If you do not have a GHIN, there are several free apps that will calculate a handicap for you. The Grint and Swing by Swing are a few good ones.

Handicaps will not be adjusted based on performance during the trip. Rather, a course handicap is calculated for each round based on your initial index and the course rating and slope of the tees you elect to play. A course handicap is calculated as (H.I. x (slope / 113)) + (course rating - course par), rounded to the nearest integer. For example, if your index is 8.2 and you play from the Regular tees at Pine Needles (6,002 yards, 68.6 rating, 126 slope) your course handicap would be (8.2 x (126 / 113)) + (68.6 - 72) = 5.743 = 6. However, if you decided to play the Medal tees at Pine Needles (7,035 yards, 73.5 rating, 134 slope) your course handicap would be (8.2 x (134 / 113)) + (73.5 - 72) = 11.224 = 11. Each player will select the tees they wish to play from for each course prior to the trip. Mid-trip changes to your selected tees are not allowed

Please review each course scorecard and select the tees you wish to play. Send Stuart your selected tees by May 12th.

Side Games

Skins are based on net low score, and are $5 per round ($30 total) and then split among the participants based on the number of skins won. Please note that if your handicap is greater than 18, for the skins game (and only for the skins game) your handicap will be adjusted to 18. Closest to the pin is $10 per round ($60 total) and all par 3s each round will have a winner. The payout will be based on the number of par 3s each round

Teams

At the end of every round the 8 players will be randomly split into teams of two. The buy-in for this game is $10 per round ($60 total). The top two two-man teams with the lowest net combined score will win the cash prizes accordingly:

First place: $50

Second place: $30

Ties will result in the splitting of the relevant prizes. For example, a two-way tie for first will see those two teams each winning $40 ($50 + $30 = $80 / 2 = $40).
